Your Perfect Haidian Date Plan
============================

Designed for couples who cherish meaningful moments, quiet beauty, and shared discovery — this plan blends academic charm, imperial serenity, and gentle spirituality — all within 5 km of central Haidian.

📍 Location 1: Peking University (PKU)
• Address: No. 5 Yiheyuan Road, Haidian District (entrance near Summer Palace West Gate)
• Why it’s perfect: Free, open all day, and brimming with poetic atmosphere — Weiming Lake’s shimmering waters, stone bridges draped in willows, cherry blossom lanes (late March–early April), and golden ginkgo tunnels (mid-October–early November). Yannan Garden — where scholars like Cai Yuanpei and Bingxin once lived — feels like stepping into a love story from another century.
• Romantic highlights:
  – Sunrise or late-afternoon light over Weiming Lake (ideal for photos & quiet conversation)
  – Find the "Love Lock" bridge near Boya Pagoda
  – Sit on the lakeside bench facing the pagoda — watch ducks glide and students cycle past

📍 Location 2: Wofo Temple (Temple of the Reclining Buddha)
• Address: Inside Beijing Botanical Garden, Wofosi Road, Haidian District
• Why it’s perfect: A place of stillness and reverence — home to a 600-year-old bronze Reclining Buddha, ancient courtyards, and fragrant cypress trees. Less crowded than major temples, yet deeply atmospheric — ideal for reflection, gentle hand-holding, and shared awe.
• Romantic highlights:
  – Stand together before the Reclining Buddha (symbolizing peace & impermanence — a beautiful metaphor for love)
  – Walk the "Three Places and Six Courtyards" — find the quietest eastern courtyard for a private moment
  – Capture golden-hour photos at the temple’s main gate, framed by red walls and pine boughs

⏰ Your Ideal Itinerary (Saturday/Sunday, Spring or Autumn):
9:30 AM — Meet at PKU’s South Gate (or East Gate if coming from Zhongguancun). Stroll through Shuchun Garden → cross the stone bridge to Weiming Lake.
11:30 AM — Coffee break at “Zhi Chun Lu Café” (just outside East Gate) — try their jasmine tea latte and almond cake.
1:00 PM — Take Bus 331 (5-min walk from PKU East Gate) to Beijing Botanical Garden South Gate (15 min ride). Enter and walk 10 mins to Wofo Temple.
2:30 PM — Explore the temple grounds mindfully: Bell & Drum Tower → Four Heavenly Kings Hall → Reclining Buddha Hall → serene eastern monks’ courtyards.
4:00 PM — Exit via the garden’s lakeside path — pause at the small pavilion overlooking the water. Watch the light soften, share a quiet moment, and snap your favorite photo.

🚇 Transport Tips:
• PKU: Nearest metro — Line 4 Zhongguancun Station (Exit D), then 10-min walk or bike.
• Wofo Temple: From PKU — Bus 331 (dep. East Gate stop) → get off at “Beijing Botanical Garden South Gate”. Or taxi (~¥15, 12 mins).
• Both sites are <5 km apart — easily covered by Didi or shared e-bike.

✨ Pro Tips:
• Best photo spots: PKU’s Boya Pagoda reflection + Wofo Temple’s red gate with pine framing.
• Bring: Light jacket (evenings cool), reusable water bottle, and an open heart.
• Bonus: If visiting in spring, bring a small notebook — sit by Weiming Lake and write each other one sentence about what you admire most in the other.

— Because love, like Weiming Lake, is deepest when still… and like the Reclining Buddha, most beautiful in its gentle, enduring presence.
